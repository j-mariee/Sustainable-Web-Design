Your Digital Footprint: The Hidden Energy Consumption of Websites and how Sustainable Web Design can Help Reduce it

Context

In an ever-growing digital society, the internet has grown to be an indispensable everyday tool. However, unbeknowst to the majority of the world, the internet has also become an immense significant contributor to electricity consumption. If the internet were its own country, it would be the sixth most polluting one in the world (Greenwood, 2021). To combat the every growing energy consumption of the internet, a new practice called sustainable web design has taken root.

What is Sustainable Web Design

Sustainable web design is a practice where web designers take action to reduce the web's energy consumption. As the creators of web content, it is the responsibility of web designers to learn how to implement sustainable web design into their workflow. In "Your Digital Footprint" issues such as inefficient data centers and excessive web content are highlighte. Solutions covered for inefficent data centers include hyperscale data centers and green hostings. For excessive web content presented solutions range from minimizing images and video, which colors are the most energy efficent, to typography.

Following the guidlines of sustainable web design, the full report of 'Your Digital Footprint: The Hidden Energy Consumption of the Websites' is presented via a simple html and css website. Images, colors, and typography are kept to a minimum to decrease the website's page weight and overally energy consumption.
